Module Summary

Soil-Transmitted Helminthiases (STH) affect the poorest and most deprived communities. The WHO recommends periodic treatment with anthelminthic (deworming) medicines to all at-risk people. This module demonstrates the direct clinical efficacy of Albendazole and the critical importance of WASH (Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene) practices to break the transmission cycle.

Clinical Case Summary

Chief Complaint: A 5-year-old male from a rural community is brought to the PHC. Mother reports child passing "white thread-like structures" in stool. Shows lethargy, poor appetite, and pica.

Examination: Conjunctival pallor noted. Abdomen is slightly distended but non-tender.

Diagnosis: Soil-Transmitted Helminthiasis (Likely Ascaris lumbricoides).

Management Protocol:

  • Albendazole 400 mg (chewable tablet) stat.
  • Iron folic acid (IFA) supplementation for anemia correction.
  • WASH Counseling to prevent reinfection.
