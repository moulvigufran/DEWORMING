National Deworming Day (NDD) Guidelines & Pharmacology

Mechanism of Action

Albendazole is a broad-spectrum anthelmintic. It exerts its effect by binding to the colchicine-sensitive site of tubulin, thereby inhibiting microtubule polymerization in the parasite.

This selective mechanism impairs glucose uptake, depletes glycogen stores, and drastically decreases ATP production, resulting in flaccid paralysis and eventual death of the helminth.

History & Strategy

Launched in 2015 by the MoHFW, Government of India, NDD is the world's largest public health program for deworming.

Conducted bi-annually on February 10th and August 10th, it targets children aged 1-19 years to reduce the burden of Soil-Transmitted Helminths (STH), improving cognitive development, nutrition, and school attendance.

Clinical Case Application

Real-world scenario evaluation.

Pediatric Presentation

Patient: 5-year-old male | Setting: Rural PHC

Chief Complaint

Mother reports the child has been passing "white thread-like structures" in stool. The child exhibits marked lethargy, poor appetite, and pica (frequently eating dirt).

Examination & Diagnosis

Significant conjunctival pallor noted (signs of anemia). Abdomen is slightly distended but non-tender.
Diagnosis: Soil-Transmitted Helminthiasis (Likely Ascaris lumbricoides).

Management Plan

  • Pharmacotherapy: Albendazole 400 mg (chewable tablet) stat.
  • Supplementation: Iron Folic Acid (IFA) syrup for associated anemia.
  • Counseling: WASH (Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene) education for parents to prevent reinfection. Emphasize handwashing and wearing footwear.
